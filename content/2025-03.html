Why Did He Turn? 🖊️
London Review of Books—March 20, 2025
A look at Mario Vargas Llosa's rightward drift since his days as a young socialist agitator in the 1960s. Evokes an era of Soviet intellectual, ideological, and artistic entanglement with the world, and offers a microcosm of recent Latin American political history.

Nuanced and thought-provoking exploration of the state of sex and desire and the social and political forces shaping them.

Birkbeck Institute for the Study of Antisemitism—March 12, 2025
A lecture by Yale historian Maurice Samuels on his recently published Alfred Dreyfus: The Man at the Center of the Affair. An excellent introduction to the Dreyfus Affair and its lasting implications.
Frequencies of Deceit: How Global Propaganda Wars Shaped the Middle East 🎙️
New Books Network—March 8, 2025
How Arab, British, American, and Soviet regimes used radio to shape public opinion in the Middle East during the 1950s and '60s. This world of radio is one I knew through stories—my aunts came of age during this era—and I loved hearing a more in-depth account on it.
