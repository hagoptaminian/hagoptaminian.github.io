It's 1958, and Nedra and Viri are a young couple living in suburban New York. They have two daughters and bohemian friends; they host dinner parties and go to the theater. It appears to be a happy enough life... but this is mid-century America, and suburban idylls are fracturing. Nedra, especially, yearns for more, and Viri's meek contentedness is bound to disappoint her. Hers is a courageously undertaken but ultimately impossible search, while Viri's basic decency and acceptance of constraints come to seem increasingly benighted. A compelling and readable book on how China's top-down "engineering state" pulls off feats of building that the more "lawyerly" U.S. increasingly struggles to match.
