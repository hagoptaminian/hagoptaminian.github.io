Can you rewire your brain? 🖊️
Aeon—February 3, 2026
What the metaphor of 'rewiring the brain' gets wrong, and clues to how changes in the brain actually happen, from introducing novel environments and experiences to repeating new behaviors. One upshot of the piece is that change is often slow and non-linear, and therefore painful. And while old patterns may never completely disappear, they can be gradually replaced by cultivating alternative neural pathways.
Mexistentialism 🖊️
Aeon—February 17, 2026
On the wisdom of 'Mexistentialism', a detached fatalism—nada es seguro—exemplified by the writer's Mexican father, whose experience of the indeterminacy of life shapes a philosophy of taking nothing for granted. Building on his father's example, the author suggests that, contrary to a Eurocentric pursuit of objectivity, philosophy may ultimately be inseparable from lived experience.
