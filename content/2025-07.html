Fred Smith: An American Life 🖊️
Arena Magazine—June 23, 2025
An ode to Fred Smith, the founder of FedEx, who died in June 2025. Evokes a bygone era of old-school, brick-and-mortar entrepreneurship, where businesses were built slowly, over decades, with minimal hype.
Walk in these 🖊️
Aeon—June 30, 2025
On the multitudes of meaning behind the shoes we wear. Little noticed, they say a lot about us: Where we walk, what we do for work, what 'tribes' we belong to. They anchor our memories (think of how an old pair of shoes transports you in time) and our metaphors (you 'step into another man's shoes').
Something Extraordinary Is Happening All Over the World 🖊️
New York Times—January 31, 2025
A thoughtful, thought-provoking piece on human migration. I was surprised to learn that, despite the headlines, more than 96% of the world's population lives in the country it was born in. "People have moved for many reasons, but always because they sought something they wanted that they could not get at home. It's an act of faith, fundamentally, kindled by the fire of human aspiration."
The Future Of Health On A Damaged Planet 🖊️
Noema—July 10, 2025
An excellent piece on how good health ('salutogenesis') is not just an individual outcome but one rooted in the community and broader planet. Its components include a perception that the world is, if not stable, then predictable; that you can make plans with reasonable reliability; that you have the resources to adapt or cope with challenges; and that the world broadly makes sense.
The Ascendance Of Algorithmic Tyranny 🖊️
Noema—July 1, 2025
A piece arguing that paradigms of modernity shape social, cultural, and political life. Industrial modernity inspired an era of top-down, hierarchical organization, and though it delivered prosperity in the form of rising living standards, stable jobs, and increasing wages, it came with tyrannies of its own, such as its stifling of individualism and stultifying uniformity. Today's dominant paradigm, digital modernity, promises liberation from precisely those tyrannies: it celebrates individualism and decentralization. But it comes with tyrannies of its own: the dominant platforms and their algorithms nudge us this way and that, shaping our social reality. Perhaps most perniciously, they undermine our capacity for ambitious collective projects and decision-making.
Finding Space To Dissent From Israel’s Wartime Narrative 🖊️
New Lines Magazine—July 14, 2025
Writer and journalist Lisa Goldman reflects on a short documentary film she watched on a visit to Tel Aviv in May 2025. The film chronicles the experience of a young conscript who serves three years as an army photographer at a base in the Negev Desert, raising, in Goldman's words, "penetrating questions about universal concerns like generational trauma and why we perpetuate cycles of hatred and wars."
The History of Bad Ideas: Identity 🎙️
Past Present Future—July 17, 2025
David Runciman in conversation with Alexander Douglass, author of the recently published Against Identity: The Wisdom of Escaping the Self. Deep and nuanced, the discussion reveals how the ways in which we determine our identities have changed over the past several centuries. Where most of us were once born into pre-existing roles, and our task was largely to perform them, today many of us are expected to have an 'authentic', self-articulating identity—which we paradoxically construct, Douglass suggests, by emulating others.
